UCL Working Papers in Linguistics 14 (2002)  Even if and even: The case for aninferential scalar account CORINNE ITEN This paper considers two central questions: (a) whatis the general nature of the meaning encoded by even? and(b) what exactly is the meaning even encodes?  A wide variety of answers to these twoquestions, proposed in the linguistics and philosophy of language literature,are considered and it is argued that none of them are entirely satisfactory. Asan alternative, an analysis is proposed according to which even encodesa procedural constraint on the context in which the utterance is to beprocessed.[PDF file]
