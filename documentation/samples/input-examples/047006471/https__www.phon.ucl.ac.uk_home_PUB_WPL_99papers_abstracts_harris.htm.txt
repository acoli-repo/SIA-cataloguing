UCL Working Papers in Linguistics 11 (1999) Release the captive coda: the foot as adomain of phonetic interpretation JOHN HARRIS The set of prosodic conditions on the phonetic interpretation of consonants canbe advantageously constrained by abandoning the device of ambisyllabicity.Supposedly ambisyllabic regularities can be straightforwardly detailed byreference to their location within the independently necessary domain of thefoot. Drawing on illustrative data from Danish and Ibibio, the paper outlines atheory of segmental form which allows explicit statements to be made about howfoot-internal effects map onto the acoustic signal.
